Moreover, Honnold, most importantly, exercises falling.

The climber claims he has been desensitized to falling since climbing without a rope requires pushing yourself to the point of failure, and fear prevents you from pushing yourself to the limit.

When he doesn’t have any planned major climbs, he usually concentrates on strengthening his fitness base by sprinting up mountains.

Alex Honnold | Charity

Alex Honnold | Movies, Books, and Endorsement

Movies

Alex starred in “Valley Uprising,” a 2014 documentary that captured the development of rock climbing in Yosemite Park.

Clif Bar was one of the project’s financial sponsors and had sponsorship agreements with five participants, including Honnold.

However, a few months after the movie’s debut, Clif Bar ended its sponsorship agreements with the climbers.

They claimed that they made this decision because the corporation didn’t like how far the climbers were pushing the activity’s limits and thought they were taking unnecessarily high risks.

Alex is renowned for taking tremendous risks for his life and safety while maintaining a seemingly carefree attitude.

He used the 2,900-foot Freerider route to make the first free solo ascent of El Capitan on June 3rd, 2017.

His achievement has been hailed as one of the most impressive athletic accomplishments ever. He finished the ascent in 3 hours and 56 minutes.

Jimmy Chin, a climber and photographer, and his wife Elizabeth Chai Vasarhelyi, a documentary filmmaker, documented it.

In 2018, they released the documentary film “Free Solo.” That year, it took home the Oscar for Best Documentary Feature.

Alex has also been featured in television series like “Billions” (2022)” and “Duncanville” (2020).

His new virtual reality movie, “Alex Honnold: The Soloist VR,” was released in 2022.

The movie allows fans to follow along as he does free-solo climbs in the French and Italian Alps.

Books

In 2015, Honnold published his first book, “Alone on the Wall,” which explains his career, struggle, and achievements.

Likewise, Honnold’s second book is the 2019 “El Capitan: The Greatest Rock Climb in the World!”

Alex Honnold | Career

Even though he had competed in climbing for much of his life, until roughly 2007, Alex was hardly known outside the climbing world.

He free soloed Astroman and Rostrum in Yosemite Valley that year, a feat only surpassed by climber Peter Croft in 1987.

Only after this success, he started to receive greater attention from the climbing community.

The following year, he free soloed a 1,200-feet-tall finger crack that separates Zion’s Moonlight Buttress.

When this accomplishment was announced, many people assumed it was a joke because of the climb’s great difficulty and the date of the announcement, which was on April 1.

Later in 2008, Honnold free soloed Yosemite’s Half Dome’s 2,000-feet-tall Regular Northwest Face.
