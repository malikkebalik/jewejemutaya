Money Heist Korea Actor Lee Hyun Woo Wiki | Bio

Lee Hyun Woo (born on March 23, 1993) is a 29 years old Korean actor popularly known for portraying the role of Ri Hae-jin in the 2013 film “Secretly Greatly.” Wyun was only 13 when he started his career as an actor. His first project was a show titled “Spring Days.” But he later worked in films too. If you have watched “Geunyeoneun Geojitmaleul Neomoo Saranghae,” you might have seen him playing the role of Kang Han-Kyeol.

Recently Hyun became a world-famous celebrity because he played the role of a hacker named Rio in the Netflix series “Money Heist: Korea – Joint Economic Area.” The story of this show is based on a group of people who together capture the Korean Mint to steal 4 trillion Won.

Early Life

The South Korean actor Lee Hyun Woo (age 29) took birth on March 23, 1993, in Anyang, South Korea. One day Hyun was mimicking a movie scene, and his mother got so impressed that she decided to send him to an acting school. Within a few years, Woo became a polished artist and started working in shows and movies.

Although he tasted success early, he kept attending school regularly like other kids. In 2011, after completing high school, Woo applied to study theater and film at Dongguk University.

Career

After debuting through a show, Lee worked in two movies, “A Dirty Carnival” and “Holli-Dei,” but he had to wait 5 years to get a role in his third film. During that time, he mainly worked on tv programs, namely “Dae Wang Sejong,” “The Return of Iljimae,” “The Great Queen Seondeok,” “God of Study,” “Gye Baek,” and “Brain.”

Finally, in 2011, he returned to work on the big screen with a film named “Glove,” a sports drama where Jae-Yeong Jeong played the lead role of a baseball player named Kim Sang-Nam. With time Lee’s name got added to the most popular young Korean artists.

He gave back-to-back award-winning performances in productions such as “Jeogdoui Namja,” “To the Beautiful You,” “Moorim School,” “Saranghae” and “Money Heist: Korea – Joint Economic Area.”

Besides movies and shows, he also appeared in music videos such as “Jump,” “I Love You,” “Ordinary Love,” and IU’s “Above the Time.” In 2015, Lee’s ability received recognition after being presented with the 52nd Grand Bell Award in the Best New Actor category and the 30th Korea Best Dresser Swan Award in the Rising Star category.

Personal Life

In 2019, IU and Hyun got spotted by some people outside a restaurant a few days after the release of the “You and I” music video. Rumors were all over the internet about their affair, but nothing was proved.

After IU, Woo’s name was attached to Pyo Ye Jin, and later the couple accepted that they started dating. Sadly, this relationship got broken by the end of 2019. So the good news for the female fans of Lee is that their favorite movie star is single.
