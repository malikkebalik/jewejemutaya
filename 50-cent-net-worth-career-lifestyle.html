50 Cent | Net Worth, Income & Bankruptcy 

Net Worth

One of the best-selling rappers of all time, 50 Cent’s net worth as of December 2023 is a whopping $40 million.

50 Cent has invested in many different projects, but one of the best ventures he did was with Vitamin Water when he converted his fame for a minority stake in return for being a celebrity spokesperson and offering his name for the drink “Formula 50”.

The Coca-Cola company later acquired Vitamin Water from Glaceau for $4.1 billion, making 50 Cent approximately ten times more money than he ever made from music.

Initially, it was said that 50’s equity stake in Vitamin Water was 10% which would have meant $410 million, but the numbers were widely dismissed by investment bankers, and said that 50’s stake was close to 2.5%.

This means he made $150 million before tax from the acquisition.

Moreover, 50 Cent launched his champagne line Le Chemin du Roi, which means “for winners only,” in 2010 with a gold-plated cross on every bottle costing $1,000 a bottle.

To date, rapper and entrepreneur 50 Cent has earned a little more than $261 million from his various pursuits, but with his extravagant spending spree, he lost almost all his money.

Furthermore, in October 2018, the rapper signed a deal with Starz, the cable home to his drama show “Power.”

The four-year deal, which includes a three-series commitment, could be worth $150 million if all terms and conditions are achieved.

Rapper 50 Cent has been rumored to have a minority stake in Effen Vodka, a Holland-based company.

Although he was rumored to have sold his stake for $60 million, it was later denied by the company’s owner Beam Suntory.

No further news on the deal has been out yet but considering his bankruptcy filing, he might have sold his apparent stake.

Income

As mentioned before, 50 Cent has earned hundreds of millions of dollars from his various pursuits.

However, his yearly income slowly decreased from that point, with average annual earnings of $4 to $5 million.

50 Cent | Lifestyle

Curtis went to Andrew Jackson High School but got arrested for possession and selling of drugs. 

When he was 19 years old, an undercover police officer arrested him for selling cocaine, and three weeks later, when his house was raided, police found heroin and crack and $15,000 in cash.

However, he still went back to dealing but knew his luck would run out eventually. It was time for a radical change of plan.

Inspired by rappers who turned their greedy street stories into cold-hearted cash Curtis came up with new hustle.

In 1996, he had a chance to meet with one of Run DMC members at a Manhattan nightclub. Curtis recorded a rough demo track for Jam Master Jay.

The track was raw but very real. Seeing the potential, Jam Master Jay signed Curtis to his label, and just like that 21-year-old small-time crack dealer had a new career.

He named himself “50 Cent” as a metaphor for change. And even bigger change came to his life a year later when 22 years old Cent and his girlfriend, Shaniqua Tomkins, had a baby boy, Marquise Jackson.

50 Cent | Career

Curtis Jackson, a small-time drug dealer from Queens, New York, became a recognized rapper after being discovered by Eminem and Dr. Dre in 2002.

Eminem was so astonished by the 50’s track “Guess Who’s Back?” that he invited Jackson to LA to meet Dr. Dre. 50 signed a $1 million record deal and released “No Mercy, No Fear.” 

Jackson gained massive popularity after releasing his debut album “Get Rich or Die Trying.” It debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard charts and sold more than a million copies in its first four days.

Once the richest rapper in the whole world, his bank accounts took hits several times over the years.

In 2007, he had an estimated net worth of $150 million, which later plunged and left him nearly broke. However, 50 is the kind of man who knows how to make money and has pulled himself out of the financial mess.
